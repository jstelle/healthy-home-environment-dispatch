What Are Some Things You Can Do to Reduce the Risk of Injuries in Your Home?

Injuries are unfortunate and unpredictable. You never know when you might hurt yourself or someone else in your home. Even with the best safety precautions, accidents can happen anywhere. Fortunately, there are many ways to reduce the risk of injury in your home. The following tips can help minimize the risk of injury in your home by keeping you and your family safe. Make sure you understand your home’s layout and know where things are kept. Avoid hazards in the home by keeping sharp objects and chemicals in a locked cabinet or drawer. Install safety equipment like childproof latches on cabinets or drawers or a safety gate at the top of the stairs. Keep items in high-risk areas out of reach of curious children and pets. Store items like cleaning supplies or poisons in a locked cabinet or drawer. Install safety gates at the top of the stairs to keep children from falling down them. Use covers on electrical outlets to prevent small children from putting things in them that could cause an electrical fire. Place electrical cords out of children’s reach. Install smoke alarms in all bedrooms and outside each doorway to bedrooms. Place carbon monoxide alarms outside each bedroom and near the appliances that produce it.
